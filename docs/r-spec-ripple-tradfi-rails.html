Ripple/XRP and the TradFi rails — the documented core, the SEC outcome, Hidden Road/Ripple Prime, and the ISO 20022 / SWIFT / JPM / BIS / IMF / World Bank claims (documented vs rumored, dated)

1. The documented core (fact)

Ripple Labs (fka OpenCoin, 2012), co-founded by Chris Larsen + Jed McCaleb + Arthur Britto, with David Schwartz (CTO), Brad Garlinghouse (CEO), Stuart Alderoty (CLO/President of legal); it controls a large share of the 100B XRP premine via on-ledger escrow. Products: the XRP Ledger (XRPL), Ripple Payments / ODL (On-Demand Liquidity, formerly xRapid), and RLUSD (a USD stablecoin, NYDFS-approved Dec 2024). Fact.

2. The SEC outcome (fact, dated)

SEC v. Ripple (filed 22 Dec 2020): Judge Torres's 13 Jul 2023 summary judgment held Ripple's institutional XRP sales were unregistered securities offerings, but programmatic/exchange sales and distributions were not — the much-cited split. Aug-2024 remedies: a $125M civil penalty + injunction. Both sides appealed; a proposed settlement to cut the penalty (~$50M) and lift the injunction was blocked by Judge Torres (2025); the parties then dropped their appeals (Aug 2025), leaving the 2023 ruling and $125M penalty as the final word. Fact (the failed $50M-settlement attempt noted, dated).

3. The prime brokerage (fact)

Ripple's institutional push: it acquired prime broker Hidden Road for $1.25B (announced 8 Apr 2025; closed Oct 2025) — becoming the first crypto firm to run a global, multi-asset prime brokerage (rebranded Ripple Prime; Hidden Road also won a FINRA broker-dealer license). Part of an acquisition streak (also Rail, GTreasury). Fact.

4. The ISO 20022 claim (GRADED — the central red herring)

SWIFT completed its migration of FI-to-FI cross-border messaging to ISO 20022 (MT→ISO coexistence ended 22 Nov 2025). ISO 20022 is a data/messaging standard — it defines how payment messages are structured; it does not "certify", list, or endorse any cryptocurrency. The community "ISO 20022 coin list" (XRP/XLM/HBAR/QNT/XDC/ADA/ALGO) is a marketing/community construct, not an ISO or SWIFT designation. Ripple markets XRP as a bridge asset within RippleNet's ISO-20022-ready stack and is "technically aligned" — but that is alignment, not adoption-by-banks. Grade: "ISO 20022 = banks will use XRP" is weak/unsupported; the standard cutover is fact.

5. JPM + the institutions (what they actually do)

Refuting "they run on XRP": JPMorgan built its own bank deposit token + blockchain unit — JPM Coin, now Kinexys — not XRP-based. The BIS (and its CPMI) promotes ISO 20022 for cross-border payments and runs CBDC/tokenization pilots (mBridge, Agorá) on bespoke DLT, not XRPL. The IMF and World Bank promote ISO 20022 in payment-system modernization/development; the "IMF SDR will be XRP" claim is unverified — the SDR is a fiat-currency basket, not crypto. Ripple has participated in WEF digital-asset/CBDC working groups (participation, not institutional adoption). Institutional facts documented; the XRP-adoption versions unverified/refuted.

6. The honest reading

The documented Ripple↔TradFi picture is substantial but narrower than the lore: a real (partial) SEC win, a real prime-brokerage build-out (Hidden Road/Ripple Prime), a real stablecoin (RLUSD), and genuine technical alignment with the ISO 20022 standard global finance is adopting. What is not documented — yet widely asserted as if it were — is that SWIFT, JPMorgan, the BIS, the IMF, or the World Bank settle on XRP, or that ISO 20022 "certifies" it. The standard cutover (22 Nov 2025) is fact; the leap to "banks now use XRP" is the red herring. Per #171's discipline, the absence of a public XRP integration at these institutions is treated as absence of evidence, not proof of a hidden one. Documented and rumored kept separate, dated.

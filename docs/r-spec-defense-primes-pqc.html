1. The US primes + the Raytheon settlement

Lockheed Martin is the largest US prime (F-35, PAC-3, space); RTX holds Raytheon (+ Pratt & Whitney, Collins). In Oct 2024, Raytheon paid >$950M (DOJ) + ~$124M (SEC) for three schemes: Qatar foreign bribery (~$2M sham subcontracts + $30M+ to a Qatari-Emir-relative agent, FCPA), defective pricing on Patriot + radar to the DoD (admitted), and export-control fraud (~$230.4M criminal + $36M forfeiture; whistleblower-aided). Fact (adjudicated/admitted).

2. The Leonardo structure (state champion behind a FOCI firewall)

Leonardo S.p.A. (formerly Finmeccanica) is Italy's defense champion, ~30% state-owned via the MEF. Its US arm, Leonardo DRS, must run under a DoD FOCI proxy agreement — cleared US-citizen proxies hold the voting rights so the Italian parent can't direct classified US programs. FOCI is the standard US firewall for foreign-owned defense suppliers (allied-but-foreign ownership mitigated, not barred). Fact.

Bribery history: the AgustaWestland India VVIP-helicopter scandal (~€560M) saw ex-chairman Orsi and Spagnolo prosecuted in Italy and India (outcomes varied by jurisdiction/appeal — graded carefully, not "all convicted"). The pattern (state-champion arms exporter + intermediary bribery in import markets) rhymes with the Raytheon-Qatar case.

ELSAG: Leonardo's ALPR (automatic license-plate reader) arm sells mass vehicle-tracking hardware to US law enforcement — so one entity spans state-owned arms manufacture AND domestic mass surveillance (into the private-surveillance complex; consumer-rights/privacy concerns). Products fact; mass-surveillance read graded.

3. The QRL / XMSS post-quantum patent thread

CONFIRMED (primary-source bibliographic data): US20240048369A1, "Quantum resistant ledger for secure communications" (application US18/358,378, filed 2023-07-25, published 2024-02-08, status pending) is assigned to Lockheed Martin Corporation (assigned 2023-08-07; inventors Ambrose Kam & Alexander Richard Vesey; classification H04L9/0852 Quantum cryptography; also filed GB/CA/AU). The method applies a Quantum Resistant Ledger (QRL) encryption scheme to data by security level and communicates the encrypted data between network components.

So a top US defense prime holds secure-comms IP built on QRL — a defense-industrial-base endorsement of the QRL/XMSS post-quantum approach, consistent with CNSA 2.0's PQC-migration mandate (hash-based XMSS/LMS per NIST SP 800-208). The graph draws Lockheed Martin → patent → QRL. Caveat: a pending application is a documented filing, not proof of deployment in fielded systems. Assignee + filing fact; operational use not claimed.

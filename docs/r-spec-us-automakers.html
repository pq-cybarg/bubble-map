1. The incumbents (fact)

Ford (strong ICE/hybrid + Pro commercial; its Model e EV unit lost ~$5B/yr, prompting 2024-25 EV pullbacks/retiming), General Motors (Ultium battery platform; BrightDrop; Super Cruise), and Stellantis (14 brands incl. Jeep/Ram/Dodge/Chrysler + EU brands) — whose CEO Carlos Tavares was ousted (1 Dec 2024) amid a US inventory glut, dealer revolt, and sales slump; Antonio Filosa became CEO (2025). Fact.

2. The Cruise wind-down (fact)

After an Oct-2023 incident in which a Cruise vehicle dragged a pedestrian (and California suspended its permits), GM cut funding and in Dec 2024 announced it would wind down Cruise as a robotaxi business, folding the tech into GM for personal-vehicle driver-assist. Fact.

3. The EV-natives + backers (fact)

Tesla (volume EV leader; Cybertruck; FSD; launched an Austin robotaxi service, 2025); Rivian (R1T/R1S + coming R2/R3) — Amazon is its largest shareholder + EDV van customer, and Volkswagen Group formed a JV (announced 2024, up to ~$5.8B) to use Rivian's software/electrical architecture; Lucid (Gravity SUV) — majority-backed by Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund (PIF, ~60%), high cash burn / low volume. Fact.

4. NACS + the Musk overhang

Tesla's standard win: Tesla opened its connector as the North American Charging Standard (NACS), and in 2023-24 Ford, GM, and nearly all other US makers adopted NACS for future EVs + Supercharger access — a proprietary design becoming the industry standard. The overhang: Elon Musk's political activity (incl. the 2025 DOGE role) is widely linked to Tesla brand damage + a 2025 sales softening — recorded both ways (a real reputational/sales effect vs Tesla's structural EV lead). NACS adoption fact; the Musk-brand effect is documented but its magnitude is contested.

Both documented.

Strategic Lenses

The frameworks this project reads through — so you can read the strategy yourself. Theirs and ours.

Every block in this investigation is read through a small set of lenses: old strategic texts that explain how contested systems are actually won, and the epistemic discipline that keeps the reading honest. Two ideas recur. The decisive variable is legitimacy / trust, not arms or money. And the cheapest way to win is to erode a rival's cohesion, alliances, and strategy — not to fight its army. These are interpretive lenses and graded interpretation, not claims of proven coordinated intent. Depth versions: Strategic reading lenses & the block-by-block re-read.

I. The classical lenses

Sun Tzu — 孫子兵法 (The Art of War)

"To subdue the enemy without fighting is the acme of skill." (不戰而屈人之兵)

WhatThe Five Factors open with 道 — the Way (whether the people are in accord with the ruler: legitimacy/cohesion), before timing, position, command, and method. Win by attacking the enemy's strategy and alliances, not its army; use economy of force; and prize foreknowledge (ch. XIII, the Use of Spies) as the decisive asset.
TheirsThe calibrated rare-earth "faucet" + predatory pricing is subdue-without-fighting — bankrupt rivals with no shooting war; foreknowledge of competitors' costs and timelines times the strike (Molycorp's weaknesses were in its public filings).
OursThe modern inversion of the spy chapter: Sun Tzu needed spies because foreknowledge was scarce; open societies now broadcast their dispositions (filings, publications, conferences), so the foreknowledge edge accrues for free, without spies. The answer is therefore structural — deny the value — not hunt an undetectable individual.
→ rare-earth statecraft (competitive intelligence)

The OSS Simple Sabotage Field Manual (1944)

"…make faulty decisions, adopt an uncooperative attitude, and induce others to follow suit."

WhatA declassified OSS manual that weaponizes normal-looking behavior — insisting on proper channels, referring matters to committees, perfectionism, exaggerated caution — to degrade an organization deniably. The genius is that the sabotage is indistinguishable from ordinary diligence.
TheirsThe template for deniable friction: slow a rival's progress while looking merely thorough or risk-averse.
Ours — the cautionThat same indistinguishability makes it an offensive tool and a useless, dangerous detection lens: "they're cautious, therefore sabotage" is unfalsifiable. We use the manual to understand the playbook — never to accuse an individual (that way lies the China-Initiative profiling error).
→ research-security & tech-transfer

Confucius — 論語 (the Analects)

"必也正名乎 — what is necessary is to rectify names." (13.3) · "民無信不立 — without the people's trust, [the state] cannot stand." (12.7)

What正名 (rectification of names): if names are not correct, language does not accord with the truth of things, and nothing can succeed. 12.7: a state may give up arms, then food, but trust (信) is the one thing it cannot do without. 2.3: lead by virtue, not coercion.
TheirsControl through corrupted names — euphemism that forecloses debate ("age verification," "core socialist values," "for the children"): name a control rail as protection and you cannot argue about it as control.
OursThe project's own method — evidence-grading, refusing fabricated names, judging the claim not the source — is 正名, the Confucian defense. Trust/legitimacy is the center of gravity the whole map keeps returning to.
→ the block-by-block re-read

II. Our discipline (the epistemic lenses)

The classical lenses say what to look for; these keep the looking honest — and are applied to ourselves as strictly as to anyone else.

Galaxy-brain resistance / the unfalsifiability guard

"If your arguments can justify anything, then your arguments imply nothing." (Buterin, 2025)

RuleA theory that explains every observation — and every absence of evidence — explains nothing. The moment "no evidence" gets recoded as "proof of sophistication," the claim has become unfalsifiable and must be set down (as a permanent open possibility, not a finding).
→ Vitalik's corpus · → verification collapse

The composition / division fallacy guard

RuleDon't give a collective a unitary mind (composition: "China wants…") or impute the whole's traits to an individual (division: suspecting a person by nationality). Institutional action ≠ institutional mind; a documented national-capability transfer ≠ any individual's intent. An advisory lint in audit.py flags collective-intent phrasing.

Verify, don't trust (trust models)

RuleMinimize and distribute trust; rely on open math and architecture, not on trusting — or blanket-distrusting — any actor. Where intent is unrecoverable, the defense is intent-independent: reproduce before relying; reintroduce backpressure; deny the single point of failure.

The verification-collapse law

RuleWhen producing a plausible artifact becomes cheaper than verifying it, every open/trust-based system floods — science, AI evals, scientific publishing, open source, security, and even a live face on a video call. "Looks legitimate" goes free; "is legitimate" stays expensive.
→ the reproducibility/integrity collapse

The hard-money lens

RuleRe-denominate value in gold- and silver-ounces at the time of measure, and report the gold/silver ratio — to separate real gains from debasement (a US home is ~−81% in gold since 1998). An overlay for perspective, never part of the formal proofs.
→ the gold/silver reprice

Evidence grading & the proof core

RuleEvery claim is graded fact · contested · weak · unsupported; intent is never inferred from adjacency; and only the explicitly-marked, machine-verified core (Z3 / TLA+ / Alloy) carries the weight of "proven" — everything else is interpretive overlay.
→ methodology
The convergence: across all of these, the contested variable is legitimacy / trust (信 / 道), and the project's own refusal to misname things is the defense it describes.

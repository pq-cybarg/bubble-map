Planet Labs & the commercial space-imaging / Earth-observation industry — operators, the NRO/NGA demand side, launch, and the 2025-26 consolidation (documented, dated)

Frame. The commercial Earth-observation (EO) layer that feeds US/allied intelligence and the AI-analytics economy. Planet Labs (NYSE: PL) runs the largest daily-scan fleet (Dove/SuperDove) plus tasking (SkySat→Pelican) and hyperspectral (Tanager). The field churned hard in 2025-26 — Maxar → Vantor + Lanteris (Oct 1 2025, Advent-owned); Capella → IonQ (Jul 15 2025); HawkEye 360, SpaceX, and Firefly IPO'd; Spire sold AIS to Kpler; Albedo exited imagery. Demand is bifurcated: the NRO buys the pixels (EOCL), the NGA does analytics/distribution (Luno; the GEGD portal; the Ukraine surge). Discipline. IDIQ "ceilings" are competed maxima, not guaranteed revenue; "launched" ≠ "operational"; headline GAAP losses can be non-cash. Facts are sourced; secondary/aggregator figures and forward guidance are flagged. Overlay; excluded from the proofs.

1. Planet Labs (fact)

Co-founders Will Marshall (CEO) + Robbie Schingler; gov work via Planet Labs Federal, Inc. Constellations: PlanetScope (Dove/SuperDove, daily ~3m 8-band, continuously replenished — 36 launched Jan 2025 + 36 Nov 2025); SkySat (~50cm tasking, ex-Google Terra Bella 2017); Pelican (SkySat successor, ~50→30cm, ~8 on orbit by May 2026); Tanager (hyperspectral/methane with Carbon Mapper; Tanager-1 Aug 2024, 11,000+ plumes); "Owl" (announced Oct 7 2025, near-daily 1m, onboard NVIDIA GPUs). AI pivot: Anthropic/Claude (Mar 2025), NVIDIA "GPU-native AI engine" (Mar 2026, ran inference in space on a Pelican Apr 2026), Planet Insights Platform. FY2026 (ended Jan 31 2026): revenue $307.7M (+26%), first full-year positive Adj EBITDA +$15.5M, GAAP net loss $(246.9)M of which ~$161.4M is a non-cash warrant revaluation, cash+ST $640.1M, backlog $900.4M (+79%). Fact (FY26 via 8-K; SkySat op-count & FY27 guidance flagged secondary).

2. Planet's contracts & ownership (fact; the "one big NGA check" premise is wrong)

Biggest deals are commercial/allied-sovereign, not one NGA award: SKY Perfect JSAT (Japan) $230M (Jan 2025, dedicated ~10-sat Pelican — commercial, not JAXA); Germany €240M/~$280M (Jul 2025, security); Sweden low-nine-figure sovereign military (Jan 2026). US intel: NRO EOCL (2022, initial ~$146M — an initial award, not a 10-yr ceiling); NGA Luno B slot (Jan 2025, 8-figure task orders). Ownership: Alphabet/Google largest single holder (~10-11%, legacy Terra Bella — flag: verify % vs 13F/proxy).

  • BlackSky (BKSY): Gen-3 35cm on Rocket Lab; Spectra AI; NRO EOCL ~$1.02B; Luno A/B.

NRO SCE by phenomenology (SAR: Capella/ICEYE US/Umbra to Jan 2027; RF; hyperspectral; EO; new CSO approach 2026). NGA Luno A ($290M, Sept 2024, 10 vendors) + Luno B ($200M, Jan 2025, 13 vendors incl. 5. Launch layer & consolidation (fact)

SpaceX (SPCX, IPO'd Jun 2026): Falcon 9 + Transporter rideshare = EO-smallsat workhorse; Starshield = NRO proliferated recon. Rocket Lab (RKLB): Electron = dedicated small-EO/SAR launcher; moving up-stack (Neutron slipped to Q4 2026; $816M SDA prime; bought Geost; own constellation planned). ULA: Vulcan halted after a Feb 12 2026 SRB anomaly (CEO Bruno → Blue Origin). Blue Origin: New Glenn booster landing Nov 2025, then an LC-36 pad explosion May 28 2026. Firefly (FLY, IPO'd Aug 2025; Blue Ghost lunar landing Mar 2025). Sierra Space ($550M Series C at $8B, Mar 2026). Consolidation: RF SIGINT → HawkEye 360 (absorbed Aurora Insight/Maxar RF + ISA); SAR bifurcating (Capella→IonQ; ICEYE selling sovereign systems); EU imaging/manufacturing → the Airbus-Leonardo-Thales "Bromo" entity.

6. The honest reading

Commercial space-imaging is now a strategic ISR layer: a few EO operators (Vantor, Planet, BlackSky), SAR operators (Capella/ICEYE/Umbra), and RF (HawkEye 360) sell into a bifurcated US demand structure — NRO imagery (EOCL/SCE) + NGA analytics (Luno) distributed via GEGD — and the Ukraine war (and the Mar 2025 suspend-then-restore) shows how load-bearing and policy-contingent the commercial layer has become. Planet's niche is daily global coverage + a fast AI/analytics pivot, now at its first full year of positive Adjusted EBITDA. Three recurring traps the sources themselves flag: (1) IDIQ "ceilings" ≠ revenue (Maxar's $3.24B is not Planet's ~$146M); (2) "launched" ≠ "operational" (ICEYE 62 launched; Jilin-1 117 claimed vs ~63 trackable); (3) GAAP losses can be non-cash (Planet's warrant revaluation; Satellogic's Tether-note swings).

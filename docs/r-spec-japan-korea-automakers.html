Japan + South Korea automakers — Toyota's hybrid bet + the cert-fraud scandals, the collapsed Honda-Nissan merger, and Hyundai/Kia (both sides, dated)

Threads: Toyota's contested hybrid-over-BEV strategy; the 2023-24 certification-fraud scandals (Daihatsu/Toyota/Hino); the Honda-Nissan merger (Dec 2024) that collapsed (Feb 2025); Renault's loosened Nissan alliance; Suzuki's India dominance; Hyundai's EV push + US Georgia Metaplant. 1. Toyota's hybrid bet (both ways)

Toyota — the world's largest (+ Lexus/Daihatsu/Hino; keiretsu suppliers Denso/Aisin) — bet on hybrids + a multi-pathway (HEV/PHEV/H2) strategy over a BEV-only pivot (chair Akio Toyoda). (A) 2021-23 — criticized as a BEV laggard (one weak bZ4X; environmental groups graded it last). (B) 2024-25 — looked vindicated as global BEV growth cooled and hybrids sold strongly, lifting profits. Both documented; the verdict shifted with the market.

2. The certification-fraud cluster (both ways)

(A) The violations are real — Daihatsu (Toyota's small-car unit) admitted decades of safety/crash-test certification fraud (Dec 2023), halting production; Toyota Industries, Hino, and then Toyota Motor itself disclosed type-certification irregularities (2024), pausing some models. (B) Toyota's framing — many were old/edge-case testing-procedure deviations and the vehicles met or exceeded actual safety standards (re-tests largely confirmed compliance). The fraud/irregularities are fact; "systemic danger vs procedural deviations on safe cars" is contested.

3. The Honda-Nissan merger (fact)

In Dec 2024 Honda + Nissan (Mitsubishi as observer) signed an MOU to explore a merger that would have created the world's ~#3 automaker — then collapsed in Feb 2025, reportedly after Honda proposed making Nissan a subsidiary rather than an equal. Context: Nissan's deep post-Ghosn struggles (Ghosn fled Japan, 2019), a 2024-25 restructuring with major job cuts, and reported Foxconn interest. Fact.

4. The rest of Japan

Mazda; Subaru (Toyota owns ~20% + co-develops EVs); Suzuki (dominant in India via Maruti Suzuki, ~40% share — cross-link India); Mitsubishi Motors (alliance member). Renault rebalanced its long Nissan alliance in 2023 (cutting its cross-shareholding) — the loosening that preceded Nissan's pivot toward Honda. Fact.

5. Korea — Hyundai Motor Group

Hyundai + Kia + Genesis — a chaebol controlled by the Chung family. Strong EV execution (the E-GMP platform; Ioniq/EV6) and a big US bet: the Georgia "Metaplant" (HMGMA, ramping 2024) tied to IRA localization. Stains: 2022-23 reports of child labor at US parts suppliers. Fact.

6. The two sides (both ways, dated)

  • (A) Strength. Toyota's hybrid discipline is paying off; Hyundai/Kia are among the best global EV executors; Japanese quality/scale endure.
  • (B) Stress. The cert-fraud scandals dented the "Japanese quality" brand; Nissan is in genuine distress (failed merger, cuts); Japanese makers are behind in China (losing share to BYD); Hyundai carries supplier-labor stains.

Both documented.

7. The honest reading

A split: Toyota's contested hybrid bet (laggard 2021-23, vindicated by 2024-25) and Hyundai/Kia's strong EV execution on one side; the 2023-24 certification-fraud scandals (Daihatsu's decades of fraud is the hard case; Toyota's own irregularities the softer one), Nissan's distress + the collapsed Honda-Nissan merger, and China-share losses on the other. The cert-fraud and Toyota-strategy questions are presented both ways with dates; the merger collapse and Hyundai facts are clean.

1. The series (fact)

Three BPI reports by Sam Lyman: Part I Foreign Influence in the Campaign Against American AI (2026-05-18); Part II Singham Ground Game (2026-06-29); Part III China-Aligned Ground Game Stalled or Blocked $23.6 Billion in American AI Infrastructure (2026-06-29). BPI is a 501(c)(3) (EIN 88-1567390).

2. The Singham network — documented core (fact)

Neville Roy Singham: US citizen based in Shanghai; sold Thoughtworks (~$785M); self-described Marxist; prior Huawei consulting. Per NYT (2023-08-05), he worked from a Shanghai office shared with a Chinese media firm and attended a CCP propaganda workshop; the NYT traced hundreds of millions to a network mixing progressive advocacy with Chinese-government talking points. A Delhi Police chargesheet (May 2024) named the Chinese government the network's "ultimate paymaster." Fox News Digital's reading of IRS Form 990s (Mar 2026) put ~$278M from Singham-controlled entities into six US nonprofits (2017-23): The People's Forum, ANSWER Coalition, BreakThrough News, Tricontinental Institute, Justice and Education Fund, CodePink (co-founded by Jodie Evans, Singham's spouse). Sen. Rubio referred Singham entities to DOJ for FARA review (2023); reporting describes a DOJ grand-jury probe. Fact. Singham's response (2023): he is "not a member of, work for, take orders from, or follow instructions of any political party or government."

3. The ground game (participation fact; direction graded)

PSL (a Marxist-Leninist party) shares leadership with the Singham-funded nonprofits — De la Cruz, Garcia, the Beckers, Puryear (ex-Radio Sputnik), De Los Santos, Benjamin, La Riva. PSL and allied groups took part in ~21 data-center-opposition campaigns in 14 states (canvassing, petitions, packed councils; slogan "America's Dairyland, not America's Data Land"). Outcomes attributed at least partly to this activity: ~10 local moratoria (Prince George's County MD, Charlotte NC, Madison/Dane County WI, DeKalb/Athens-Clarke GA, Durham/Cumberland NC, Denver CO, Box Elder UT), a permanent ban (Monterey Park CA, June 2026, 86%), and 4 rejections/withdrawals (Tucson/Chandler AZ; a $1B Google project in Franklin Township IN; a $12B QTS/Blackstone campus in DeForest WI). Fact that these groups ran/joined the campaigns. Causation hedged by the report: "The PSL's role in each of these campaigns ranged from lead organizer to one member of a broader coalition."

4. The broader claim (mixed)

Part I widens the aperture: (a) Chinese state media (CGTN/China Daily/Global Times) ran attributed anti-data-center items while China subsidizes its own AI-datacenter energy — the report reads the asymmetry as intent; (b) Russian RT promoted data-center bans in ME/NY/SC/OK; (c) foreign billionaires Wyss (Swiss, ~$700M) and Parker (British, Oak Foundation >$750M) fund US advocacy; (d) a 230+ org coalition letter (Dec 8 2025) preceded a Sanders bill ~107 days later; (e) Singham-funded Tricontinental opposed US chip export controls echoing China's Foreign Ministry. Grade: mixed — the state-media items and grants are documented, but Wyss/Parker are Swiss/British funders of broad progressive causes (folding them into "against American AI" is a stretch), and parallel messaging ≠ shown coordination.

5. The $23.6B figure (weak)

Part III's headline number has no stated methodology and names only three dollar-figured projects (Prince George's MD $5B; DeForest WI $12B; Monterey Park CA ban, unspecified) — summing to ~$17B, with the remainder unexplained. Grade: the specific blocked projects are real; the $23.6B aggregate is unverified.

6. Publisher interest (read words-for-intent on the framer)

The Bitcoin Policy Institute is a pro-Bitcoin / pro-AI-infrastructure think tank, so it has a direct interest in recasting data-center opposition as foreign-influenced rather than legitimate local dissent. That doesn't falsify the documented facts (corroborated well beyond BPI), but the selection and causal framing — which opposition counts, how much agency to assign the network — should be read as advocacy and graded independently.

7. Composition & causation guard

The error to avoid: treating "documented Singham funding + CCP-aligned content + PSL participation in some campaigns" as "the American anti-data-center movement is a Chinese operation." The reports themselves disclaim it — Part II: "This report does not assert that members of the PSL are Chinese operatives"; Part III: "Americans have genuine concerns... These concerns are authentic," with a foreign campaign running "parallel to this domestic, democratic movement." Genuine drivers (electricity prices, water, noise, land, tax abatements) are real and widespread. Warranted findings: (i) a real CCP-aligned Singham network exists; (ii) its activists genuinely joined specific data-center fights; (iii) whether that directed (vs joined/amplified) the movement is unproven, and the aggregate-impact figure is unsubstantiated.

8. The honest reading

There is a documented, CCP-aligned influence network around Singham (NYT 2023; IRS 990s ~$278M; Delhi "ultimate paymaster" 2024; Rubio FARA referral; reported DOJ grand-jury probe), and activists from that ecosystem genuinely took part in ~21 US data-center fights that produced moratoria, a ban, and withdrawals. That is fact. BPI's bigger claim — a coordinated foreign influence campaign that blocked "$23.6B" — is graded: the reports hedge causation, the dollar figure has no method, and the author is a pro-AI institute. By the composition guard, the finding is a real network + real participation + parallel messaging — not proof that foreign direction originated or controls the opposition. By read-words-for-intent, the network conspicuously does not disclose its financing (PSL is exempt from public reporting) and echoes CCP positions — which cuts toward alignment; but non-disclosure + alignment is not command. Network facts and report hedges both retained, with dates. Overlay; excluded from the proofs.

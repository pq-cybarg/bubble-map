Singapore — the hedge-state financial hub: GIC/Temasek, MAS crypto, PSA transshipment, the S$3B laundering bust, and Chinese wealth inflows (both sides, dated)

Frame. Singapore as the archetypal hedge-state — a US security partner and a China economic node, and Asia's premier wealth/financial hub. Threads: the two sovereign funds (GIC ~$770B est., Temasek ~$300B+), MAS's crypto licensing (tightened after FTX/3AC/Terra all had Singapore links), PSA (world's busiest transshipment port), the family-office/private-banking boom drawing Chinese capital flight — and the S$3B money-laundering bust (2023). 1. The funds

GIC (the sovereign wealth fund, est. ~$770B AUM) + Temasek (the state holding company, ~$300B+; took a ~$275M FTX writedown in 2022) make the city-state a top-tier global allocator. Fact.

2. The crypto regime

MAS courted then tightened crypto: the 2022 blowups all had Singapore links (Three Arrows Capital Singapore-based; Terraform/Do Kwon ties; FTX touched Temasek), after which MAS tightened licensing + retail curbs (2023-25) and pushed firms offshore. Fact.

3. Port + wealth

PSA runs the world's busiest transshipment port (a trade chokepoint). Singapore is also Asia's wealth-management hub — a surge of single-family offices + private banking, heavily fed by Chinese capital flight / de-risking. Fact.

4. The laundering bust (both ways)

In Aug 2023 Singapore busted a ~S$3B (~$2.2B) money-laundering ring — 10 Fujian-origin individuals (foreign passports), cash/property/crypto/luxury seized — one of the world's largest. (A) enforcement caught it + tightened rules; (B) the scale showed how much illicit Asian capital the hub had absorbed. Fact.

5. The hedge

Singapore hosts US military rotations/logistics and is deeply integrated with China's economy — the textbook hedge (cross-link #144). Fact.

6. The honest reading

Singapore is the hedge-state financial hub: world-class sovereign funds (GIC/Temasek) + transshipment (PSA) + a wealth/crypto center that courted then disciplined crypto (FTX/3AC/Terra links) and absorbed Chinese capital flight — until the 2023 S$3B laundering bust exposed the AML cost. The clean-hub-vs-laundromat tension is presented both ways; the US-security/China-economy hedge is the structural point. Overlay; excluded from the proofs.

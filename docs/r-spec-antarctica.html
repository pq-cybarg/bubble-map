1. The Treaty

The Antarctic Treaty (1959, in force 1961) reserves Antarctica for peaceful/scientific use, demilitarizes it, bans nuclear tests, and freezes the 7 claims (no new claims; none recognized/renounced). The Antarctic Treaty System governs by consensus. Fact.

2. The mining ban

The 1991 Madrid Protocol bans all mineral activity except research — a "natural reserve" — reviewable from ~2048 (the system's stress test). Fact.

3. The claims

Seven claimants: UK, Argentina, Chile (overlapping on the Peninsula), + Norway, Australia, France, New Zealand. The US and Russia recognize no claims but reserve the right. The UK/Argentina/Chile overlap mirrors the Falklands/Malvinas friction. Fact.

4. The competition (both ways)

China opened its 5th station (Qinling, Ross Sea, 2024) — dual-use/SIGINT concerns vs stated science; Russia's Rosgeo reported very large hydrocarbon "seismic surveys" (Weddell Sea, 2020s) — framed as research but read by critics as prospecting breaching the mining-ban spirit; fishing pressure (krill, toothfish) + tourism + biosecurity stress the CCAMLR regime. Bases/surveys are fact; "research vs prospecting / dual-use" is contested.

5. The honest reading

Antarctica's governance (1959 Treaty + 1991 mining ban) still holds, but the consensus is fraying: overlapping UK/Argentina/Chile claims, China's growing base network (dual-use questions), Russia's hydrocarbon "surveys" (research-vs-prospecting), and mounting fishing/biosecurity pressure — with the ~2048 mining-ban review looming as the real test. Facts dated; the research-vs-resource reading is presented both ways; no unitary national "mind" imputed. Overlay; excluded from the proofs.

The Arctic — the resource race, the Northern Sea Route, Greenland, and the militarization (both sides, dated)

Built 2026-06-29 from research/spec-arctic.json. Regional block. Cross-links Russia, China, US_Government, NATO, Canada, BRI.

Frame. The warming Arctic as a resource + shipping + military arena. Threads: hydrocarbons + rare earths + fisheries opening up; the Northern Sea Route (Russia) vs the Northwest Passage (Canada); militarization (Russia's rebuilt bases; Finland + Sweden joining NATO 2023-24; the US push to acquire Greenland, 2025); the Arctic Council paralyzed after Russia's 2022 invasion; China's self-styled "near-Arctic state" + Polar Silk Road. Discipline. Resource/shipping/militarization facts, the Council paralysis, China's posture, and the US Greenland push are fact; motives are not imputed. Overlay; excluded from the proofs.

1. Resources

Warming opens oil/gas (a large share of undiscovered reserves), rare earths/critical minerals (Greenland), and fisheries — a contested-governance frontier. Fact.

2. Routes

The Northern Sea Route along Russia's coast (Russia-controlled, China-courted) vs the Northwest Passage through Canada's archipelago (Canada claims internal waters; the US calls it an international strait). Fact.

3. Militarization

Russia has the longest Arctic coastline + rebuilt bases; Finland (2023) + Sweden (2024) joined NATO (→ ~7-of-8 Arctic states NATO + Russia); the US operates Pituffik (Thule) in Greenland and in 2025 pressed to acquire/control Greenland (a Danish territory) — a major flashpoint. Fact.

4. Governance

The Arctic Council was effectively paralyzed after Russia's 2022 invasion (the 7 others suspended cooperation), weakening the main forum as competition intensified. China styles itself a "near-Arctic state" + pursues a Polar Silk Road (BRI). Fact.

5. The honest reading

A resource + shipping + military arena heating up literally and geopolitically: opening hydrocarbons/minerals/fisheries, the Russia-controlled NSR vs Canada's Northwest Passage, a near-fully-NATO Arctic facing Russia, China's "near-Arctic"/Polar-Silk-Road entry, and the US's 2025 Greenland push — all while the Arctic Council sits paralyzed. Facts dated; no unitary national "mind" imputed. Overlay; excluded from the proofs.

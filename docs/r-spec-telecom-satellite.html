The surveillance proof: Salt Typhoon broke the mandated backdoor

China's MSS-linked Salt Typhoon infiltrated ~9 US carriers (Verizon, AT&T, T-Mobile, Spectrum, Lumen, Consolidated, Windstream) from ~2021 — and compromised the CALEA lawful-intercept system itself, the court-authorized wiretap apparatus US law requires carriers to maintain. It became a foreign-intelligence open door: real-time location tracking, call recording, and the metadata of 1M+ users; FBI counts 200+ orgs across 80 countries. "The worst telecom hack in American history," and officials say the actors may persist despite eviction claims.

This is the cleanest empirical proof of the repo's anti-backdoor thesis. A surveillance capability the state mandated became the exact vector of catastrophic compromise. It generalizes age-verification-abolition's "if IDs are ever breached, IDs become meaningless" to the wiretap layer, and continues the Snowden/Bullrun crypto-weakening lineage (Task #7). You cannot build a backdoor only the good guys can use.

# String of Pearls — China's Indian-Ocean port/base network, the framing dispute, and the Hambantota "debt-trap" correction (both sides, dated)

*Built 2026-06-28 from `research/spec-string-of-pearls.json`. Maritime extension of the corridors block (#143). Cross-links `China`, `India`, `BRI`, `COSCO`, `Infrastructure_Corridors`.*

> **Frame.** The network of Chinese-built/operated ports + a base ringing the Indian Ocean. **Key discipline:** "String of Pearls" is a **US/India strategic-analysis term** (Booz Allen, 2005) for an **encirclement** reading; China frames the same ports as **commercial** (the Maritime Silk Road / BRI). Both framings recorded; **intent not imputed**. The **Hambantota** 99-year lease (2017) — the canonical "debt-trap" example — is **corrected** per the scholarship.
>
> **Discipline.** The ports/base + dates + the Hambantota lease are **fact**. The "debt-trap seizure" reading is **corrected** (Chatham House / Brautigam). Overlay; excluded from the proofs.

## 1. The pearls (fact)

- **Gwadar** (Pakistan) — the CPEC flagship deep-water port on the Arabian Sea.
- **Hambantota** (Sri Lanka) — leased **99 years** to China Merchants Port (**2017**, ~$1.1B).
- **Djibouti base** — China's **first overseas military base** (opened **2017**), beside the US's Camp Lemonnier.
- **Kyaukpyu** (Myanmar) — deep-sea port + oil/gas **pipelines** giving China an Indian-Ocean outlet **bypassing the Malacca Strait**.
- **Piraeus** (Greece) — **COSCO**'s majority-owned Mediterranean gateway into Europe.

Others frequently cited: Chittagong (Bangladesh), the Maldives, Port Sudan. *Fact.*

## 2. The framing (both ways, no imputed intent)

- **(A) Encirclement reading** (US/India analysts) — a "String of Pearls" of **dual-use** ports that could become naval footholds around India (evidenced by the Djibouti base + PLA-Navy port calls).
- **(B) Commercial reading** (China) — Maritime-Silk-Road trade/energy logistics (esp. the **Malacca-bypass** via Gwadar/Kyaukpyu pipelines), not bases.

The honest middle: most are **commercial ports with latent dual-use potential**; only **Djibouti** is an actual base to date. *Both documented; "naval-base network" is contested beyond Djibouti.*

## 3. The Hambantota correction (myth, corrected)

The story that China "seized" Hambantota when Sri Lanka couldn't repay a loan is **not** what the record shows:

1. Sri Lanka's 2017 debt distress was driven mainly by **Western capital-market sovereign bonds (ISBs)** + a strong dollar — **Chinese debt was a minority share**.
2. The port was **never loan collateral**; Sri Lanka **leased** it (99 yrs, ~$1.1B) to China Merchants to **raise foreign exchange** to pay **other** (mostly non-Chinese) debts.
3. **Chatham House** + **Deborah Brautigam**'s research conclude the "debt-trap diplomacy" framing is **largely a myth** here.

*Grade: "China seized the port for unpaid debt" is **unsupported/corrected**; the lease itself is fact.*

## 4. The India counter (fact)

India's response — a **"Necklace of Diamonds"** strategy — secures access at **Chabahar** (Iran, also an INSTC node), **Sittwe** (Myanmar), **Duqm** (Oman), **Sabang** (Indonesia), **Assumption Island** (Seychelles), plus **Quad/AUKUS** naval cooperation. Like "String of Pearls," "Necklace of Diamonds" is an **analytical label**, not an official program. *Fact (the access agreements); the label is analytical.*

## 5. The honest reading

The physical facts are clear: China built/operates a ring of Indian-Ocean ports (Gwadar, Hambantota, Kyaukpyu, Piraeus) and **one military base** (Djibouti). What is contested — recorded both ways — is whether this is an **encirclement** network ("String of Pearls," a US/India framing) or **commercial** Maritime-Silk-Road logistics (China's framing); beyond Djibouti, the "naval base" reading is not established. And the case most cited as proof of predatory intent — **Hambantota** — is corrected by the scholarship: an FX-raising lease amid a mostly-Western-bond debt crisis, not a loan-default seizure. India's "Necklace of Diamonds" mirrors the dynamic. **Intent unattributed; framings flagged; the debt-trap myth corrected.** Overlay; excluded from the proofs.

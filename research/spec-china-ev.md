## 1. The makers (fact)

**BYD** — the **global #1** (vertically integrated: own Blade LFP batteries + chips; overtook Tesla in quarterly BEV 2023-24; **Berkshire** trimmed/exited its early stake 2022-25); **SAIC** (state-owned; MG; the EU's largest CVD target); the "new forces" **NIO / XPeng / Li Auto**; **Xiaomi EV** (the phone giant's **SU7**, 2024); **Chery** (top exporter) and **Great Wall**; plus **Geely** (Zeekr/Polestar) and **Leapmotor** (Stellantis JV). *Fact.*

## 2. The price war (fact)

A brutal multi-year **price war** (BYD-led, 2023-25) amid **overcapacity** (utilization well below 100%), forcing consolidation and razor margins; in **2025** Beijing itself pushed back against destructive **"involution"** price-cutting. *Fact.*

## 3. The batteries (fact)

**CATL** is the world's #1 maker (**~37%** global share), with **BYD #2**; LG Energy Solution, Panasonic, Samsung SDI, SK On, CALB round out the field. CATL's reach is contentious in the West: it **licensed its LFP tech to Ford's Marshall, Michigan plant** (2023, later **downsized** amid pushback over Chinese-tech dependence). Battery demand cross-links the **critical-minerals/Lobito** threads (lithium/cobalt/nickel). *Fact.*

## 4. The tariffs (both ways, dated)

- **(A) Western response** — the **EU** imposed **countervailing duties** on Chinese BEVs (finalized **Oct 2024**, up to **~35%** + the 10% base; SAIC ~35, Geely ~19, BYD ~17), the **US** set a **100%** EV tariff (2024), Canada matched — citing **state subsidies + overcapacity "dumping."**
- **(B) China framing** — **protectionism** against genuine cost/scale leadership; China **retaliated** (anti-dumping probes on EU brandy/dairy/pork).

The honest middle: Chinese **subsidies and** real cost leadership both exist; the Mexico-assembly "backdoor" is a live concern. *Both documented.*

## 5. The AV/robotaxi race (fact)

**Waymo** (Alphabet) leads US paid robotaxi (multi-city); **Baidu's Apollo Go** leads China (scaled in **Wuhan, 2024**); **Tesla** launched an **Austin robotaxi** (2025); GM's **Cruise wound down** (2024, see #179); Pony.ai/WeRide are other Chinese players; **Mobileye** (Intel) supplies ADAS. *Fact.*

## 6. The honest reading

China owns the EV + battery **scale** story (BYD global #1; CATL ~37% of batteries; the largest EV market) — built on a mix of **real cost leadership and state subsidies**, both true, which is exactly why the West's tariff response (EU CVD + US 100%, 2024) is itself **dual-natured** (legitimate anti-subsidy + protectionism). The domestic **price war/overcapacity** is a genuine stress even for the winners. On autonomy, **Waymo** (US) and **Apollo Go** (China) lead paid robotaxi while Tesla pushes FSD.

# Rare-earth statecraft — China's on/off export "faucet," predatory pricing, the allied supply-response cohort, and the knowledge-transfer question

*Built 2026-06-13 from `research/spec-rare-earth-statecraft.json`. Verified: GMF (Rare Earth Statecraft), CSIS, IEA, ECB occasional paper, Small Wars Journal (predatory pricing), SCMP, Chemistry World, CRML/Ucore/REalloys press. Companion to `defense-rare-earth`, `macro-critical-minerals`.*

> **Two questions, dug deep.** **(1) Why does China keep toggling the REE faucet on and off?** — it's a *calibrated leverage instrument*, plus a decades-long *predatory-pricing* strategy. **(2) Could a China-linked researcher in Europe-funded REE research have been a vector for *European* processing know-how flowing *to* China?** — the factual substrate is now **documented from primary source**: the EU's flagship **EURARE** program *openly published* its eudialyte (the Tanbreez-class heavy-REE mineral) silica-gel-prevention process at RWTH Aachen, with a **Chinese-Government-scholarship researcher as lead experimenter** and explicit industrial scale-up intent. But that evidence **reframes** the espionage hypothesis rather than confirming it (see §3).
>
> **Grade + no defamation.** The faucet/predatory-pricing history is documented. The knowledge-transfer **factual record is documented** (a China-scholarship researcher in EU-funded, *openly published* eudialyte R&D — the authors' own self-disclosed facts). The **espionage/covert-exfiltration *intent* is unverified and is undercut by the open-publication mandate**: open publication is the antithesis of theft. **No individual is accused of any wrongdoing.**

## 1. Why the faucet toggles (the documented logic)
- **Calibrated coercion (partial > total).** Partial/targeted controls impose huge costs **while avoiding a full embargo that would trigger rapid Western substitution/reshoring** and erode leverage. The 2025 controls cut exports sharply but **did not cease** — by design.
- **Reward-compliant / punish-adversary.** A licensing regime lets Beijing approve partners and deny adversaries case-by-case (Dec 2025: foreign-military end-users denied) — export control as an active influence instrument.
- **Counter-leverage vs US chip controls.** The 2023–25 REE/gallium/germanium/antimony controls were an explicit **counter to US semiconductor curbs** — the symmetric **chips-vs-minerals** chokepoint trade.
- **Predatory price control.** The decades-long play: **lure Western firms to collaborate, then reset prices below cost to bankrupt them** (Molycorp/Mountain Pass bankruptcy, 2015). Because China controls the price, **no Western competitor's economics survive long** — so toggling supply/price keeps dependence *structural*, not just tactical.

**History:** 2010 Senkaku dispute → ~2-month REE halt to Japan (the first weaponization); 2023–24 gallium/germanium/antimony/graphite + heavy-REE controls; **Apr 4 2025** licenses on 7 heavy REEs + magnets (US-bound exports fell sharply; carmakers cut utilization; **European prices reached ~6× Chinese prices**); Nov-2025 Trump-Xi pause kept the **military-end-use denials**. China holds **~60% of mining, ~92% of processing** — the chokepoint is **separation/processing**, not ore.

## 2. The allied supply-response cohort (state-financed, racing ~2028)
- **Critical Metals Corp (CRML) / Tanbreez** — owns the **Tanbreez** REE project in Greenland (full control 2025); 10-yr offtake (~10,000 t/yr, ~10%) with **Ucore**, plus a 15% offtake with **REalloys** (BLBX).
- **Ucore Rare Metals** — **DoD/Pentagon-funded** processor building a commercial **RapidSX** separation machine in Louisiana; Ucore states RapidSX was **"built from core physics and chemistry — requiring no Chinese technology transfer at any step."**

- The same **state-as-circular-financier** structure as the broader minerals thread.

## 3. The knowledge-transfer question — *direction corrected*; factual record **documented**, espionage *intent* **reframed**
**Corrected framing (the prior pass had the direction backwards):** the hypothesis is whether a **China-linked researcher in Europe-funded REE research** could have been a vector — witting *or* unwitting — for ***European* processing know-how flowing *to* China.** *Not* "did Chinese know-how seed the West" (the reverse, which the earlier version mistakenly answered).

**The documented record (primary source — corrects the prior "no public corroboration / no individual named" line).** The EU's flagship rare-earth program **EURARE** (EU FP7-NMP-2012-LARGE-6, grant **309373**; [www.eurare.eu](https://www.eurare.eu)) *openly published* its eudialyte-processing R&D — the **dry-digestion-with-HCl + water-leach** route that solves eudialyte's central obstacle (non-filterable **silica-gel** formation) and recovers **~85–87% of total REE**, with explicit **industrial scale-up** intent (a ~100 L demonstration reactor "for an industrial application"). The work was done at the **Institute of Process Metallurgy and Metal Recycling (IME), RWTH Aachen University** (Prof. Bernd Friedrich), on eudialyte concentrate supplied by the **Geological Survey of Finland (GTK)**. **Eudialyte is the same heavy-REE mineral class as Greenland's Tanbreez** (the allied node in §2). The lead experimenter and co-author **Yiqian Ma** (IME RWTH Aachen; BSc/MSc Central South University; ex-Changsha Research Institute of Mining and Metallurgy) records in the acknowledgments that he **"is grateful to the Chinese Government for providing a scholarship."** *(Ma et al., **Metals 2018, 8(4):267**, doi:10.3390/met8040267; Ma et al., **Johnson Matthey Technol. Rev. 2019, 63(1):2–13**. These are the authors' own published, self-disclosed facts.)*

**Finding — revised.** The factual substrate is **documented, not absent**: a Chinese-Government-scholarship researcher *was* a lead experimenter in EU-funded eudialyte processing R&D aimed at industrial scale-up, on the Tanbreez-class mineral. **But this corroborates a *channel*, not a *crime*:**
1. **The know-how was *openly published*** — that is EURARE's mandate (EU public research, open-access journals). Open publication is the **antithesis of covert exfiltration**: Europe gave the eudialyte method to the world, China included, *by design*. There was no secret to steal.
2. **The realistic mechanism is normal academic diffusion** — open literature + trained-personnel mobility — now concretely instantiated, **not espionage**.
3. **Steal-incentive, refined.** The prior "weak because China leads processing (~90%)" holds for China's *dominant* feedstocks (bastnäsite/monazite/ion-adsorption clays), but is **less true for eudialyte specifically** — eudialyte's silica-gel problem was an *open research frontier* China had not productionized, so a working European eudialyte route is **genuinely novel know-how worth having**. Yet it was **given away free** via open publication, so there was nothing to take covertly. Net: *novel-and-worth-having, but openly published.*

**The sharper finding — the leak is open science, not theft.** Western policy discourse about China "stealing" rare-earth processing IP **mis-locates the diffusion**. The EU's own flagship program openly published the eudialyte (Tanbreez-class) silica-gel-prevention process, with a Chinese-Government-scholarship lead experimenter and explicit scale-up intent. To whatever extent know-how flowed China-ward, the channel was **open publication + personnel mobility chosen by EU science policy** — not covert theft. The real lever of control is **policy** (what gets open-published vs. protected; research-security around dual-use processing), not anyone's nationality or intent. The diffusion those IP-theft warnings invoke was, in this documented instance, **self-inflicted by open-science policy** — the mirror image of the complaint itself.

**Witting vs unwitting matters:** the *unwitting* version is **normal academic diffusion** (publication, collaboration, trained researchers changing jobs) — how science works, not espionage; the documented EURARE record sits squarely here. The *witting* version (deliberate, **covert** exfiltration of *protected* IP outside the open-publication channel) is the serious allegation, and it's **unverified**; intent is **not asserted** of anyone.

**No defamation.** Naming Yiqian Ma reports **only his own published, self-disclosed facts** (authorship, RWTH Aachen affiliation, the scholarship acknowledgment printed in *Metals* 2018). It is **not** an accusation of espionage, exfiltration, or any wrongdoing — none is alleged or evidenced. He is a published researcher in open EU science, nothing more.

**Critical guard:** the general dual-use research-security concern is real *as a category* — but it must **not** be generalized to "Chinese students" as a class. That's a **division fallacy** (a class-level concern doesn't transfer to any individual) **and** a discriminatory generalization; it's an institutional risk-management topic, not grounds to suspect anyone by nationality.

**What would change it:** to move from *open-science diffusion* (documented) to *covert exfiltration* (the still-unverified serious claim) would require a court filing/indictment or a documented trail showing **protected, non-published** European processing IP moved to China **outside** the open-publication channel — none found; provide such a source and it can be graded up.

# GlobalFoundries (GFS) — the AMD spinout, Mubadala ownership, the 7nm retreat + IBM suit, CHIPS money, and the DoD "trusted foundry" (both sides, dated)

> **Frame.** GF is the third-largest pure-play foundry and a rare **US-based volume fab** operator — but **majority-owned by Abu Dhabi's sovereign fund (Mubadala)**, and it deliberately **exited the leading edge in 2018** to focus on specialty/mature nodes. It is also a **DoD-accredited "trusted foundry"** and a **CHIPS Act** recipient.
>
> **Discipline.** The AMD spinout, Chartered merger, Mubadala majority ownership, the 2018 7nm exit, the IBM $1.5B handover + $2.5B lawsuit, the 2021 IPO, the ~$1.5B CHIPS award, and the trusted-foundry accreditation are **fact**. ## 1. The origin (fact)

GlobalFoundries was created in **2009** when **AMD spun out its manufacturing** (going fabless), funded by Abu Dhabi's **Advanced Technology Investment Company (ATIC**, later folded into **Mubadala**); in **2010** GF merged with Singapore's **Chartered Semiconductor**. It became the third-largest pure-play foundry (after TSMC and Samsung) and **IPO'd on NASDAQ (GFS) in Oct 2021** at a **~$47B** valuation — with **Mubadala remaining the majority owner**. *Fact.*

## 2. The 7nm retreat (fact)

In **Aug 2018**, under CEO **Tom Caulfield**, GF **abandoned its 7nm leading-edge roadmap**, judging the capital cost of competing with TSMC/Samsung/Intel at the bleeding edge uneconomic. It pivoted to **differentiated specialty/mature nodes**: RF, **FD-SOI (FDX)**, automotive, power, and silicon photonics. Both sides: critics saw the US **ceding leading-edge logic**; GF argued specialty foundry is a more defensible, profitable niche (and it has been profitable). *Fact (the exit); "retreat vs smart focus" is the read.*

## 3. The IBM suit (fact)

GF's fab footprint partly came from IBM: in **2015** IBM **paid GF ~$1.5B to take over** its chip-manufacturing (**East Fishkill, NY + Essex Junction, VT** fabs) plus IP/engineers. After GF dropped 7nm, **IBM sued GF (2021) for ~$2.5B**, alleging GF failed to deliver the 7nm/10nm chips IBM had relied on for its servers; the parties **settled** later in 2021. *Fact.*

## 4. CHIPS money + the trusted foundry (fact)

The national-security layer: GF is a **DoD-accredited "trusted foundry"** / secure US supplier (the **Essex Junction, VT** fab does RF/defense work) and it won a **~$1.5B CHIPS Act direct-funding award (Feb 2024)** to expand **Malta, NY (Fab 8)** and **Burlington, VT** for automotive, defense, and aerospace chips. So Washington is subsidizing a US fab footprint precisely for supply-chain + defense security. *Fact.*

## 5. The two sides (both ways, dated)

- **(A) Strategic-asset framing.** GF is one of very few US-based volume foundries and a DoD trusted supplier; CHIPS money is rational supply-chain + defense insurance, and specialty nodes (auto/RF/defense) are exactly where onshore capacity matters.
- **(B) Foreign-ownership concern.** A US "trusted foundry" making defense chips is **majority-owned by a foreign sovereign wealth fund** (Mubadala/Abu Dhabi), and the 2018 7nm exit means the US still depends on **TSMC/Samsung for leading-edge logic**.

*Both are real and dated; neither is adopted as the verdict.*

## 6. The honest reading

GlobalFoundries is a genuinely strategic but **ambivalent** asset: a rare US volume foundry and DoD trusted supplier that Washington is subsidizing (CHIPS) for security — yet **majority-owned by an Abu Dhabi sovereign fund** and deliberately **out of the leading edge since 2018**. Its fab base came partly from IBM (the $1.5B 2015 handover) and produced a $2.5B IBM lawsuit when GF dropped 7nm. The dated facts sit alongside an unresolved both-sides tension between "critical US capacity" and "foreign-sovereign-owned defense supplier."

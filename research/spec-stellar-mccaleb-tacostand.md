## 1. The lineage (fact)

Jed McCaleb (earlier: founder of **Mt. Gox** in 2010, sold to Karpelès 2011; and **eDonkey**) co-founded **Ripple** (OpenCoin) and was CTO until ~2013; he left amid disputes over Ripple's direction/decentralization and his role. In **2014** he co-founded the non-profit **Stellar Development Foundation (SDF)** with **Joyce Kim**. Stellar initially **forked the Ripple codebase**, then McCaleb + Graydon Hoare rewrote the consensus mechanism as the **Stellar Consensus Protocol (SCP, 2015)** — making XLM/Stellar a technically distinct, more-nonprofit-governed cousin of XRP/XRPL. *Fact.*

## 2. The settlement (fact)

McCaleb received **~9B XRP** at the XRPL's 2012 creation. After his exit, a **2014 settlement** with Ripple constrained his selling to avoid crashing the market: his (and his children's) remaining **~5.3B XRP** went into escrow with release tied to a **percentage of daily XRP trading volume**; he agreed to **donate 2B XRP** to a charitable donor-advised fund and to sell all his Ripple equity. The agreement was renegotiated over the years. *Fact.*

## 3. The tacostand (fact)

The **"tacostand"** is the community/on-ledger nickname for the **XRPL account McCaleb sold his settlement XRP from** (tracked publicly on XRPL explorers for years as a market-overhang gauge). He sold the **last of it in July 2022** — Ripple itself marked the moment with a post (*"The Stand is Finally Out of Tacos"*). So "Jed's Tacostand" is a **real, documented on-ledger wallet**, not a metaphor — and a clean example of how XRPL transparency let the community track an insider's sell-down in real time. *Fact.*

## 4. The partners (fact)

Stellar's documented institutional footprint (already in the graph, reaffirmed): **Circle** issues **USDC** natively on Stellar; the **DTCC** has run tokenization work touching Stellar; **Franklin Templeton's** on-chain US Government Money Fund (FOBXX/**BENJI**) runs on Stellar; and **MoneyGram** built a **USDC-on-Stellar** remittance / cash-in-cash-out partnership (2021–22). IBM's earlier **World Wire** cross-border product was built on Stellar (later wound down). *Fact (partner PRs); "wound-down vs active" varies by product.*

## 5. The honest reading

The McCaleb/Stellar story is well-documented and needs no embellishment: a co-founder split (2013–14) produced a technically-distinct, nonprofit-governed cousin chain (Stellar/XLM via SCP), and a settlement governed his ~9B XRP — visible to anyone on-ledger as the **"tacostand"** wallet until it emptied in Jul 2022. Stellar's real institutional ties (Circle, DTCC, Franklin Templeton, MoneyGram) are concrete and already mapped.

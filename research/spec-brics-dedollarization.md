## 1. Membership (and its contradictions)

**11 full members:** Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa + **Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, UAE** (Jan 2024) + **Indonesia** (Jan 2025). **Saudi Arabia was invited (2023) but deliberately held off** — reportedly to avoid antagonizing the US — **the cleanest single example of the bloc's internal contradiction.** Plus **10 partner states** (2025: Belarus, Bolivia, Cuba, Kazakhstan, Malaysia, Nigeria, Thailand, Uganda, Uzbekistan, Vietnam). **India chairs 2026** (18th summit, New Delhi, Sept 12-13).

The bloc is **not a unitary actor** (composition guard): India is also in the **Quad** + pursues **IMEC** (anti-BRI); Saudi/UAE are **US security partners**; the India-China **border rivalry** persists. "BRICS" names a **coalition of hedgers with overlapping rival-bloc memberships** — building **optionality**, not committing to a single front. *Fact (overlaps); "optionality" the supported inference.*

## 2. The financial architecture (parallel plumbing)

- **New Development Bank** (Shanghai) — 30% local-currency-lending target.
- **BRICS Pay** — card/settlement system for local currencies.
- **BRICS Unit** — proposed **gold-and-local-currency-backed** settlement instrument (2026 push; **aspirational**).
- **CBDC interoperability** + bilateral currency swaps.
- **Russia-China settle ~90%** of bilateral trade in local currencies, routed through **CIPS**.

*Institutions fact; deployment maturity varies (the Unit is aspirational).*

## 3. The de-dollarization reality (marginal, not collapse)

**Real at the margin, not a collapse.** The dollar still dominates reserves/invoicing/FX; BRICS has **no convertible common currency** (the yuan's capital controls cap it — the same ceiling that traps Iran's funds). What is genuinely shifting: **sanctioned states forced off the dollar** (Russia/Iran/Venezuela), **gold accumulation**, **local-currency bilateral trade**, and **payment-rail redundancy**. The trend is **fragmentation/optionality, not replacement.** *Marginal shift fact; "dollar collapse" unsupported.*

## 4. The US counter (the paradox)

Trump threatened an **additional 10% tariff** on any country "aligning with the anti-American policies of BRICS," and **100%** on dollar-reducers; **India was hit with up to 50%** (Aug 2025). The coercion is **itself a de-dollarization accelerant** for the targeted states — weaponizing the dollar incentivizes exits. *Fact.*

## 5. The honest reading

BRICS+ is a **coalition of hedging states** building **parallel financial infrastructure** (NDB, BRICS Pay, the Unit, CIPS, gold) that gives members **optionality against a weaponized dollar** — without (yet) a credible single alternative, because **members' interests diverge** and no convertible common currency exists. The structural trend: **dollar fragmentation at the margin + payment-rail redundancy, accelerated by US tariff coercion — not dollar replacement.** Stated without attributing a unitary will to the bloc (composition guard): these are the members' **separate, sometimes contradictory actions**, which aggregate into a hedge.
